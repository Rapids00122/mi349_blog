Robin Hobb was born in California on March 5th, 1952 under the name Margaret Lindholm. From a young age she had a love for fantasy writting, though it really hadn't taken off into the mainstream at the time. She started her writing career profressionally in 1983 with fantasy and sciencefiction books. In 1995 she adopted her now well known pseudoname: Robin Hobb.

It wasn't until Hobb's started the "Farseer Trilogy", beginning with "Assassins' Apprentice," that Hobb's work really started to kick off. Her work became associated with complex and interesting characters, slow methodical buildups, and compelling plots. Her books delve deep into themes of identity, loyalty, and the cost of having power.

over the years she has published 16 books in the Farseer universe. Her writting has become a pillar in the fantasy genre recieving acclaim from other massive writters like George R. R. Martin. Her stories have enchanted a vast audiance and recieved critical acclaim across the space, securing her a legacy for decades to come.

Fitz Chivalry - The Bastard

Richard Ford
Oct 27, 2023

Fitz Chivalry is the bastard son of the king in waiting, Chivalry Farseer. His mother is unknown, but he was taken away from her at a young age to be raised as a bastard in the Farseer capital city of Buck Keep. He was given over for immediate care by the keeps stable master, Burrich. Burrich had loved Fitz’s father so took to this duty heartily. Sadly, before this point Chivalry had been unable to produce an heir with his current wife, Lady Patience. The drama that unfolded following the discovery of his bastard son caused Chivalry to abdicate his king in waiting position and move away from the capital – never to see his son. Shortly after his seclusion he died under mysterious circumstances.

Fitz was raised by Burrich and learned all manner of skills in animal husbandry. Over time, Burrich and Fitz developed a father son relationship. As he got older, Fitz started to draw more attention around the capital. Having a bastard roaming about was a precarious thing in those days. The current king, King Shrewd, made quick to secure Fitz loyalty. He made sure Fitz was well taken care of and started to train him in all manner of skills from sword fighting, court manners, map making, and more. His most useful training came in the form of his training in assassination and magic. For you see, Fitz was particularly skilled in the Skill (this settings magic). It allows one’s wielder to reach into the mind of other people and influence them. Combine that with his quick wits makes for an able assassin.

So, Fitz is now loyal to King Shrewd and does whatever he must to protect him, the royal family, his friends, and the kingdom.

Burrich - The Stable Master

Richard Ford
Oct 27, 2023

Burrich is a steadfast and loyal character in "Assassin's Apprentice." He serves as the stable master at the keep, where he is known for his unwavering dedication to his duties and those under his care. He was deeply loyal to his previous master and king in waiting, Chivalry Farseer. His last command given to him by Chivalry was to care for young Fitz as best he could. He has a strong connection with all manner of animals.

Burrich's rugged appearance, combined with his gentle yet firm nature, makes him a trusted figure in young Fitz's life. He plays a significant role in shaping Fitz's character and becomes a fatherly figure to the young protagonist, offering guidance and support as Fitz navigates the challenges of the world, he finds himself in.

Burrich is a very matter of fact no beating around the bush personality. He believes in hard work and dedication and doesn’t like those who shirk their duties. This helps to mold Fitz into becoming a very strong working character who doesn’t mess around. This greatly improves his position in the court as others look to him for help and the king begins to give him more respect and responsibilities.
